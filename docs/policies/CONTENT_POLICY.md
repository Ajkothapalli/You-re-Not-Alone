# Content Policy — You Are Not Alone

**Last updated:** [DATE]

This page describes what content is allowed on You Are Not Alone, how we
enforce our rules, and how to report something that shouldn't be here.

---

## What is never allowed

### Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)

Zero tolerance. No exceptions.

Any submission that our automated system identifies as CSAM is blocked before
storage, the account is immediately and permanently banned, and a report is
submitted to the [NCMEC CyberTipline](https://www.missingkids.org/gethelpnow/cybertipline)
and applicable law enforcement authorities. We do not retain the content.

### Targeting real people

You may not submit content that:
- Threatens, harasses, or targets an identifiable real person.
- Includes another person's private information (address, phone number,
  intimate images, etc.) without their consent.

### Hate and incitement

Content that promotes hatred, discrimination, or violence based on race,
ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or national
origin is not allowed.

### Other prohibited content

- Graphic violence or gore.
- Spam, advertising, or commercial solicitation.
- Content designed to probe or circumvent our safety systems.
- Facilitation of illegal activity.

---

## What is allowed

You Are Not Alone is a space for writing about personal experience — however
heavy or complicated. The following is explicitly permitted:

- Confessions about grief, loss, anxiety, trauma, relationship pain, shame,
  or loneliness.
- Writing about your own experiences with abuse, addiction, or mental health
  struggles.
- Difficult emotions expressed in the first person.

Our moderation is designed to protect people from targeted harm, not to
silence difficult personal expression.

---

## How enforcement works

### Before your confession is stored

Every submission passes through a three-layer automated safety check on our
server before it is stored or shown to anyone:

1. **Content moderation** — An AI classifier checks for prohibited categories.
   Flagged submissions are blocked and never stored.
2. **Crisis detection** — A keyword list and an AI classifier check for
   indications of crisis (suicidal ideation, self-harm, severe distress).
   Crisis submissions are not published. You are shown crisis resources instead.
3. **CSAM screening** — A dedicated classifier checks for child safety signals.
   Any match results in blocking and mandatory reporting (see above).

This pipeline is mandatory. It cannot be skipped, and it runs on our servers —
not on your device.

### After submission

**Reporting:** If you see a confession that violates this policy, tap the
"report this confession" link on the match screen. Reported confessions are
immediately hidden pending review.

**Human review:** Our team reviews all reports and unreviewed crisis events.
We aim to resolve reports within [COUNSEL: confirm SLA — e.g. 72 hours for
general content; 24 hours for CSAM/urgent content; 15 days for formal
grievances under IT Rules 2021].

**Bans:** Violations result in escalating consequences:
- Repeated violations → 24-hour suspension.
- Persistent violations → permanent ban.
- CSAM → immediate permanent ban, no appeal.

Bans operate at both account and device level.

## What we built on purpose — and why

**There are no replies, no direct messages, no profiles, and no following.**

This is a deliberate design decision, not a gap. In an anonymous app where
people share vulnerable content, any contact channel creates a vector for
targeting and harassment. We chose to close that surface entirely. Matched
users can see each other's confessions and the felt count. That is the
complete interaction.
